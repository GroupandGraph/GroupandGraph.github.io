本讨论班旨在为群论方向的青年研究者（博士生为主）提供交流群论研究与课题的平台。主题包括（但不限于）以下内容：

- 有限单群（有限单群分类定理，子群结构，共轭类等）
- 置换群及其在组合学上的应用（如传递图）
- 线性代数群与有限李型群
- 有限域与群的表示
- 有限\(p\)-群
- 计算群论
我们希望有更多的研究者参与到我们的讨论班中。如果您有意愿做报告，或者想为我们推荐演讲人选，请联系黄弘毅（邮箱hy.huang@bristol.ac.uk）。 报告内容可以是最新的研究成果，课题综述，技巧方法等。

This is a platform for young group theorists (mostly PhD students) to talk about their research and related topics. The topic can be anything related to group theory, including but not limited to the following.

- Finite simple groups (CFSG, subgroup structures and conjugacy classes)
- Permutation groups and their applications to combinatorics (e.g. transitive graphs)
- Linear algebraic groups and finite groups of Lie type
- Finite fields and group representations
- Finite \(p\)-groups
- Computational methods in group theory
If you are a young group theorist and want to give a talk, please drop an email to Hong Yi Huang at hy.huang@bristol.ac.uk. We would also love to hear any suggestions you have for speakers. Don't worry if you have no new results -- we also warmly welcome any survey, or even a method that you find helpful, on every related topic.

Time

通常情况下，讨论班的固定会议时间是北京时间周二下午16:00-18:00，由一小时报告和一小时自由讨论两部分组成。目前讨论形式为线上或线下线上同时进行。

Our usual time is Tuesday 16:00-18:00 (GMT+8). This comprises a session of a 1-hour seminar talk, and another 1-hour free discussion on any math or non-math topic. The seminar is currently held online or in hybrid style.

The Next Seminar

Title: Bases for primitive permutation groups
Speaker: 黄弘毅 Hong Yi Huang (University of Bristol)
Time: 16:00-18:00 (GMT+8), Tuesday May 24th
Location: Tencent Meeting 768 228 5095

Abstract: Let \(G \leqslant \mathrm{Sym}(\Omega)\) be a permutation group and recall that a base for \(G\) is a subset of \(\Omega\) with trivial pointwise stabiliser. Bases have been studied for many decades and an ambitious project initiated by Jan Saxl in the 1990s seeks to determine the primitive groups with a base of size \(2\). In recent years, there has been significant progress towards this goal and I will review some of the latest developments. I will also report on recent work concerning the Saxl graph of a base-two permutation group, which was recently introduced by Burness and Giudici. Some of the new results are based on recent joint work with Tim Burness.
